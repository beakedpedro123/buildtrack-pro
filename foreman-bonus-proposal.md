# Foreman Bonus Structure Proposal

## For Pedro's Framing & Steel Erection Business

**Prepared:** April 27, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

This proposal outlines a tiered foreman bonus structure designed specifically for your framing and steel erection business. The core challenge is that a flat 1% (0.01) of contract value works well for smaller jobs but becomes disproportionately expensive on jobs above $80,000. The solution presented here uses a **declining-rate tiered system** with optional **performance multipliers** that reward quality, safety, and efficiency while keeping your costs predictable.

---

## The Problem with a Flat 1%

A flat 1% bonus on contract value creates a linear cost that quickly becomes unsustainable on larger projects. The table below illustrates the issue:

| Job Value | Flat 1% Bonus | Your Concern |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| $15,000 | $150 | Reasonable |
| $30,000 | $300 | Reasonable |
| $50,000 | $500 | Reasonable |
| $80,000 | $800 | Feels too high |
| $100,000 | $1,000 | Too high |
| $150,000 | $1,500 | Way too high |
| $250,000 | $2,500 | Unsustainable |

Industry research confirms that most subcontractors use either a **declining percentage scale** or switch to **profit-based bonuses** for larger projects. According to *Construction Business Owner* magazine, foremen typically receive 1-5% of labor hours saved (not contract value), while *Ascent Consulting* recommends tying bonuses to profit improvement rather than gross revenue [1][2].

---

## Recommended Structure: Option A — Tiered Declining Rate

This is the simplest approach and the one most aligned with your current 0.01 base thinking. The bonus percentage decreases as job value increases, with a hard cap on maximum payout.

| Job Value Tier | Bonus Rate | Example Job | Bonus |
|----------------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| $0 – $50,000 | 1.00% (0.01) | $35,000 | $350 |
| $50,001 – $80,000 | 0.75% (0.0075) | $70,000 | $525 |
| $80,001 – $100,000 | 0.50% (0.005) | $90,000 | $450 |
| $100,001 – $150,000 | 0.35% (0.0035) | $130,000 | $455 |
| $150,001+ | 0.25% (0.0025) | $200,000 | $500 |

**Maximum bonus cap: $750 per job** (prevents runaway costs on very large projects).

The rate is applied to the **entire contract value** at the tier rate for that job's total value (not marginal). This keeps the math simple for foremen to understand.

**Annual cost estimate** (assuming 8-12 jobs per foreman per year):

| Scenario | Jobs/Year | Avg Job Size | Annual Bonus Cost per Foreman |
|----------|-----------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| Light year | 8 | $40,000 | ~$2,800 |
| Normal year | 10 | $60,000 | ~$4,500 |
| Heavy year | 12 | $80,000 | ~$5,400 |

## Alternative Structure: Option B — Profit-Based Bonus

Instead of basing the bonus on contract value (revenue), base it on **net profit** per job. This naturally scales because profit margins are tighter on larger, more competitive jobs. It also incentivizes your foremen to control costs, reduce waste, and avoid rework.

| Profit Tier | Foreman Bonus Rate | Example |
|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| First $5,000 of profit | 5% | $250 |
| $5,001 – $15,000 of profit | 4% | $400 |
| $15,001 – $30,000 of profit | 3% | $450 |
| $30,001+ of profit | 2% | Varies |

**Example calculation for a $100,000 job with 15% profit ($15,000):**

| Profit Bracket | Amount | Rate | Bonus |
|----------------|--------|------|-------|
| First $5,000 | $5,000 | 5% | $250 |
| $5,001 – $15,000 | $10,000 | 4% | $400 |
| **Total** | | | **$650** |

**Example calculation for a $200,000 job with 12% profit ($24,000):**

| Profit Bracket | Amount | Rate | Bonus |
|----------------|--------|------|-------|
| First $5,000 | $5,000 | 5% | $250 |
| $5,001 – $15,000 | $10,000 | 4% | $400 |
| $15,001 – $24,000 | $9,000 | 3% | $270 |
| **Total** | | | **$920** |

**Advantages of profit-based:**
The foreman is directly incentivized to reduce material waste, minimize rework, keep crews productive, and avoid equipment damage. If a job loses money, there is no bonus — which is fair. This approach is recommended by *Ascent Consulting* and *ShareWillow* as the gold standard for construction incentive programs [2][3].

**Disadvantage:** You need to share profit numbers with your foremen (at least per-job profit, not company-wide). Some owners are uncomfortable with this level of transparency.

## Performance Multipliers (Optional Add-On for Either Option)

Regardless of which base structure you choose, adding performance multipliers rewards the behaviors you actually want. The base bonus is multiplied by the applicable factors:

| Performance Metric | Multiplier | How It's Measured |
|-------------------|-----------|-------------------|
| On-time completion | 1.15x | Job completed by or before scheduled end date |
| Under budget (labor) | 1.20x | Actual labor hours < estimated labor hours |
| Zero safety incidents | 1.15x | No OSHA recordables, no injuries on job |
| Clean punchlist (< 5 items) | 1.10x | Punchlist items at final walkthrough |
| All daily reports submitted | 1.10x | 100% report compliance in BuildTrack Pro |

**Multipliers stack** but are **capped at 1.5x** the base bonus.

**Example:** Foreman earns $500 base bonus on a $50K job. He completed on time (1.15x), had zero safety incidents (1.15x), and submitted all reports (1.10x). Multiplier = 1.15 x 1.15 x 1.10 = 1.454x. Bonus = $500 x 1.454 = **$727**.

## Comparison: Option A vs. Option B

| Factor | Option A (Tiered Rate) | Option B (Profit-Based) |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|
| **Simplicity** | Very simple — foreman knows the rate | Requires sharing profit data |
| **Cost predictability** | Highly predictable with cap | Varies with job profitability |
| **Foreman motivation** | Motivated to complete jobs | Motivated to save costs AND complete |
| **Fairness on bad jobs** | Pays bonus even if job loses money | No bonus on unprofitable jobs |
| **Your recommendation** | **Start here** | Upgrade to this once trust is built |

## Implementation Recommendations

**Phase 1 — Start Simple (Now)**

Begin with **Option A (Tiered Declining Rate)** because it is easy to explain, easy to calculate, and does not require sharing profit information. Announce it at your next safety meeting and make sure every foreman understands the tiers.

**Phase 2 — Add Performance Multipliers (After 2-3 Months)**

Once foremen are used to the base structure, introduce performance multipliers. This gives you a way to reward the behaviors you care about (safety, reports, punchlist quality) without increasing the base rate.

**Phase 3 — Transition to Profit-Based (6-12 Months Out)**

Once you have enough job cost data in BuildTrack Pro and trust is established with your foremen, consider transitioning to Option B. The profit-based approach is more sustainable long-term and creates better alignment between your foremen's incentives and your bottom line.

## App Implementation Design (Future Build)

When ready to build this into BuildTrack Pro, the following database schema and features would be needed:

**Database additions:**
- `bonus_tiers` table: tier ranges, rates, caps per company
- `bonus_multipliers` table: metric name, multiplier value, measurement criteria
- `foreman_bonuses` table: jobId, foremanId, baseAmount, multiplier, finalAmount, status (pending/approved/paid), paidAt

**App features:**
- Owner: Configure bonus tiers and multipliers in Settings
- Owner: Review and approve/adjust bonuses per job on completion
- Foreman: View earned bonuses and pending bonuses on their dashboard
- PDF: Include bonus summary in Job Completion Report
- Payroll integration: Option to include approved bonuses in payroll export
